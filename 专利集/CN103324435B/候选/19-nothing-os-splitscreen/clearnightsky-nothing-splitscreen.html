How to Split Screen on Nothing Phone: A Simple Guide

March 10, 2025 by Nelson

The modern smartphone is a marvel, a pocket-sized portal to information, entertainment, and connection. But sometimes, juggling multiple apps can feel like a digital circus act. Imagine needing to check an email while simultaneously referencing a document, or comparing prices on two different shopping platforms. This is where the power of split-screen mode comes into play, and the Nothing Phone, with its streamlined user experience, makes this feature incredibly accessible.

This guide will walk you through the various methods of activating and utilizing split-screen on your Nothing Phone, ensuring you can maximize your productivity and enjoy a seamless multitasking experience. It’s about more than just opening two apps at once; it’s about enhancing your workflow and making the most of your device’s capabilities.

Understanding Split Screen

Split-screen mode, also known as split-window mode, allows users to run two applications simultaneously on the same screen. This feature is particularly useful for tasks that require referencing information from multiple sources or comparing data side-by-side. It enhances productivity by eliminating the need to constantly switch between apps, streamlining workflows and saving time.

On the Nothing Phone, split-screen mode is integrated seamlessly into the operating system, offering several intuitive ways to activate it. This ensures that users can easily access and utilize this powerful feature, regardless of their technical expertise.

Method 1: Using the Recent Apps Screen

The most common and perhaps the easiest way to initiate split-screen on your Nothing Phone is through the recent apps screen.

Steps:

1. Access the Recent Apps Screen: Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and hold briefly, or tap the square icon at the bottom of the screen, depending on your navigation settings. This will display a carousel of recently used applications.

2. Select the First App: Locate the first app you want to use in split-screen mode. Tap on the app icon located above the app preview.

3. Choose “Split Screen”: A menu will appear with several options. Select “Split screen.” The selected app will now occupy the top half of the screen.

4. Select the Second App: The bottom half of the screen will display your app drawer or the recent apps screen. Choose the second app you want to use. Once selected, it will occupy the bottom half of the screen, completing the split-screen setup.

This method is quick and efficient, making it ideal for users who frequently switch between apps.

Method 2: Utilizing the App Icon in the App Drawer

Another convenient method involves directly accessing the split-screen option from the app drawer.

Steps:

1. Open the App Drawer: Swipe up from the bottom of the home screen to access your app drawer.

2. Long Press on the App Icon: Find the first app you want to use in split-screen mode. Instead of tapping to open it normally, long-press on the app icon.

3. Select “Split Screen”: A menu will appear, offering various options. Choose “Split screen.” The selected app will then occupy the top half of the screen.

4. Select the Second App: The bottom half of the screen will display your app drawer or the recent apps screen. Choose the second app you want to use. Once selected, it will occupy the bottom half of the screen, completing the split-screen setup.

This method is particularly useful when you know exactly which apps you want to use in split-screen mode and prefer to initiate the process directly from the app drawer.

Method 3: Using the Floating Window Feature

The Nothing Phone also offers a floating window feature that, while not strictly split-screen, can provide a similar multitasking experience by allowing an app to overlay on top of another. This is useful if you need to quickly reference something without dedicating half your screen to it.

Steps:

1. Access the Recent Apps Screen: Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and hold briefly, or tap the square icon at the bottom of the screen, depending on your navigation settings.

2. Select the First App: Locate the app you want to use as a floating window. Tap on the app icon located above the app preview.

3. Choose “Floating Window”: A menu will appear with several options. Select “Floating window”. The selected app will now appear as a small, resizable window on your screen.

4. Open the Second App: Open the second app you want to use in the background. You can now interact with both apps simultaneously. The floating window can be moved around the screen and resized to your preference.

While this isn’t a true split-screen, it offers a similar level of multitasking flexibility, especially for apps where you only need to glance at information periodically.

Managing and Adjusting Split Screen

Once you have successfully activated split-screen mode, you can manage and adjust the layout to suit your needs.

  • Adjusting the Split: A black bar will appear between the two apps. Drag this bar up or down to adjust the size ratio of the two windows. This allows you to allocate more screen space to the app you’re actively using.
  • Switching Apps: To swap the positions of the apps, tap the three dots in the center of the dividing bar. A small menu will appear, allowing you to switch the apps’ positions.
  • Exiting Split Screen: To exit split-screen mode, drag the dividing bar all the way to the top or bottom of the screen. This will close the app that occupies that portion of the screen, leaving the other app in full-screen mode.

Tips for Effective Split Screen Usage

  • App Compatibility: Not all apps support split-screen mode. Some apps may not function correctly or may display incorrectly when used in split-screen. Experiment to see which apps work best for your needs.
  • Battery Consumption: Running two apps simultaneously can consume more battery power than running a single app. Be mindful of your battery level, especially when using split-screen for extended periods.
  • Screen Real Estate: While split-screen enhances multitasking, it also reduces the screen space available for each app. Consider using split-screen primarily for tasks that benefit from simultaneous viewing and interaction.
  • Keyboard Usage: When typing in split-screen mode, the keyboard may take up a significant portion of the screen, especially in portrait mode. Consider using landscape mode for typing-intensive tasks.

Questions We Hear a Lot

Here are some frequently asked questions about using split-screen on the Nothing Phone:

Q1: Why can’t I find the “Split Screen” option for some apps?

  • A: Not all apps are designed to support split-screen functionality. The app developer needs to implement the necessary code for it to work correctly. If you don’t see the “Split Screen” option, it’s likely that the app doesn’t support this feature.

Q2: Is there a way to make certain app combinations open in split-screen automatically?

  • A: Unfortunately, the Nothing Phone (and most Android devices) doesn’t have a built-in feature to automatically launch specific app pairings in split-screen mode. You’ll need to manually initiate split-screen each time.

Q3: Can I use split-screen in landscape mode?

  • A: Yes, split-screen works in both portrait and landscape modes. Landscape mode can often be more comfortable for typing or viewing content in split-screen, as it provides more horizontal screen space.

Q4: Does split-screen consume more data?

  • A: Yes, if both apps in split-screen are actively using data (e.g., streaming video, downloading files), then you will consume more data than if you were only using one app.

Q5: Is there a limit to how many apps I can have open simultaneously, including split-screen?

  • A: While there isn’t a hard limit, the performance of your Nothing Phone can be affected by having too many apps open at once, especially resource-intensive apps. The device’s RAM plays a significant role. If you notice sluggishness, try closing some background apps.
